Ada: An Offline AI Revolution for Education

Visualizing the urgent need and transformative potential of on-device AI to bridge Africa's educational divide.

The Scale of the Educational Crisis

Nigeria and Sub-Saharan Africa face a profound, multi-layered educational challenge that stifles potential and perpetuates inequality. The numbers reveal a critical need for immediate, innovative intervention.

10.5M

Out-of-School Children

Nigeria is home to 1 in 5 of the world's out-of-school children, a staggering barrier to national development.

Crippling Learning Poverty

A staggering 70% of 10-year-olds in Nigeria cannot read or understand a simple sentence, indicating a severe quality deficit in education.

1:124

Extreme Teacher Shortage

In the North-East, the teacher-to-student ratio is 1:124, drastically exceeding the 1:35 recommendation and overwhelming educators.

The Foundational Barrier: A Pervasive Digital Divide

The promise of digital learning remains out of reach for the majority due to fundamental infrastructural gaps. Ada is engineered to bypass these very obstacles.

Infrastructural & Access Deficits in Nigeria

Low internet penetration, coupled with unreliable electricity and low digital literacy, forms a formidable barrier to conventional EdTech solutions.

Regional & Gender Disparities in School Attendance

Deeply entrenched socio-cultural and economic barriers disproportionately affect girls' education, especially in Northern Nigeria.

Ada: A Self-Contained Learning Ecosystem

Ada is not just a device; it's a comprehensive, offline-first system designed to deliver high-quality education anywhere, anytime.

1

On-Device AI & Content

Full functionality with zero internet. Pre-loaded with curriculum-aligned, interactive multimedia, simulations, and virtual labs.

2

Ultra-Low Power & Durable

Engineered for just 5 watts of power and compatible with solar chargers. A robust casing ensures longevity in challenging environments.

3

Local Hub Functionality

Creates a local Wi-Fi network, allowing up to 30 users to connect their own devices simultaneously, serving entire classrooms or communities.

4

"Sneakernet" Updates

Content and analytics are updated periodically via physical storage media, ensuring freshness without reliance on the internet.

Evidence & Precedent: Offline EdTech Works

Ada builds on a foundation of proven success. Similar offline initiatives across Africa have already demonstrated significant, measurable impact on learning outcomes.

Edukite (South Africa) Matric Pass Rate Gains

Schools using the offline Edukite app saw substantially higher improvements in high-stakes exam pass rates compared to other schools.

M-Shule (Kenya)
